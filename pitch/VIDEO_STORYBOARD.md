# PUENTE — VIDEO STORYBOARD
## "El Puente" | 75 seconds
### Emotional brand film for Hack+ Alebrije CDMX 2026

---

## TONE & STYLE

- **Feeling:** Warm, grounded, dignified — never pitying
- **Cinematography:** Handheld, natural light, 16mm film grain
- **Color palette:** Golden hour warmth (Houston), deep earth tones (Oaxaca)
- **Music:** Solo acoustic guitar, traditional son jarocho feel — building slowly. No synths. No corporate music. Human hands on wood and strings.
- **Language:** Spanish with English subtitles. This is a Mexican story told in Mexican voices.
- **Reference films:** Roma (Cuaron), Sin Nombre, Coco opening sequence

---

## SCENE 1 — THE HANDS [0:00–0:15]

**Visual:** Extreme close-up of hands. Rough, calloused, cement dust in the knuckles. These hands are building something — laying brick, gripping a trowel. We hear construction sounds: hammering, a saw, boots on gravel.

**Cut to:** The same hands, cleaned up but still rough, counting twenty-dollar bills at a kitchen table. A small apartment. A framed photo of a woman on the wall. A Virgin de Guadalupe candle.

**CARLOS (V.O., in Spanish):**
> "Cada dolar que gano tiene un destino."
> *("Every dollar I earn has a destination.")*

**Cut to:** He puts the bills in an envelope. Writes "Mama" on it.

---

## SCENE 2 — THE LINE [0:15–0:30]

**Visual:** Carlos walks into a Western Union. Fluorescent lights. Linoleum floor. A line of people who look like him — tired, still in work clothes. Everyone holding envelopes.

**Close-up:** The fee schedule on the wall. $500 send = $42 fee. Carlos stares at it. He has seen it a thousand times. He reaches for his wallet anyway.

**CARLOS (V.O.):**
> "Cuarenta y dos dolares. Cada semana. Son cuatro horas de trabajo."
> *("Forty-two dollars. Every week. That is four hours of work.")*

**Visual:** His hand hesitates — then his phone buzzes. He looks down.

**On the phone screen:** The Puente app icon. A notification: "Enviar dinero a Mama — sin filas, sin comisiones abusivas."

He steps out of the line.

---

## SCENE 3 — THE BRIDGE [0:30–0:45]

**Visual:** Carlos sits on the steps outside his apartment. Golden hour. Houston skyline in the distance. He opens Puente on his phone.

**Close-up of the screen** (shot over-shoulder, natural, not staged):
- Taps "Enviar"
- Selects "Mama"
- Types "500"
- Screen shows: Fee $2.50 | Maria receives 9,714 MXN
- Confirms with fingerprint

**Visual:** A subtle, beautiful animation — light traveling across a map from Houston to Oaxaca. Not flashy. Like a heartbeat traveling through a wire. Like warmth moving through earth.

**Sound:** The guitar begins to swell. A second guitar joins — son jarocho style, call and response.

**Text on screen:**
```
4.2 segundos.
$2.50 de comision.
```

---

## SCENE 4 — THE ARRIVAL [0:45–1:00]

**Visual:** Cut to Oaxaca. A different world. Cobblestone streets. A mercado in the morning. Colorful textiles. The smell of chocolate and copal — you can almost feel it through the screen.

**Maria** is at her kitchen table. She is 61, silver-haired, strong. She is making tortillas by hand. Her phone buzzes on the table beside the comal.

**Close-up:** The notification. "Carlos te envio $9,714.25 MXN."

She smiles. Not a big dramatic smile. A quiet one. The smile of a mother who knows her son is thinking of her.

**Cut to:** Maria at the OXXO. She shows her phone to the cashier. He scans the QR. Hands her cash. She puts it in her apron.

**Cut to:** Maria walking through the mercado. She buys ingredients. She talks with a vendor — they laugh. She is not a "remittance recipient." She is a woman living her life with dignity.

---

## SCENE 5 — THE TANDA [1:00–1:10]

**Visual:** Evening. Maria's living room. Nine women sit in a circle. Coffee, pan dulce, laughter. This is the tanda meeting — but it is also community. It is also friendship.

**Maria opens Puente.** Shows the tanda screen to the group. All contributions confirmed. This month, the payout goes to Dona Lupita — she needs it for her grandson's school supplies.

**MARIA (V.O.):**
> "Siempre nos hemos cuidado entre nosotras. Ahora tenemos mejores herramientas."
> *("We have always taken care of each other. Now we have better tools.")*

**Visual:** The women raise their coffee cups. A toast between comadres.

---

## SCENE 6 — THE CLOSE [1:10–1:20]

**Visual:** Split screen. Left: Carlos on his apartment steps in Houston, phone in hand, looking at the sky. Right: Maria in her courtyard in Oaxaca, phone in her apron, watering her plants.

**They are 2,000 miles apart. They are connected.**

**Music:** Guitar resolves to a final, warm chord.

**Text on screen (white on black, clean):**

```
El ano pasado, las familias latinoamericanas
perdieron $9.3 mil millones en comisiones.

Puente lo reduce al 0.5%.

Porque el puente entre un hijo y su madre
no deberia costar nada.
```

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Duration
- Target: 75 seconds (fits 60–90s window)
- Can be trimmed to 60s by shortening Scene 2 and Scene 5

### Music
- Commission a short son jarocho piece, or license from:
  - Cafe Tacvba acoustic catalog
  - Son de Madera
  - Mono Blanco
- Alt: original composition with jarana and requinto

### Casting
- Carlos: Real construction worker if possible. Not an actor. Hands matter more than face.
- Maria: Real abuela. Warmth and strength, not fragility.
- Tanda women: Real group of friends/neighbors if possible.

### Locations
- Houston: Construction site (permission needed), small apartment interior, Western Union exterior
- Oaxaca: Mercado de Abastos or local market, residential kitchen, OXXO exterior/interior

### Filming Notes
- Shoot 4K, finish in 16mm film grain LUT
- All dialogue in Mexican Spanish — Oaxacan accent for Maria
- Subtitles in English, clean sans-serif font, bottom of frame
- No blockchain jargon anywhere in the video. Not one word.
- The technology is invisible. The human connection is everything.

### Lower-Budget Alternative
- Shoot on iPhone 15 Pro with Moment lenses
- Use real team members' families if willing
- Music: royalty-free jarana tracks from Artlist
- Edit in DaVinci Resolve (free tier)
- Total budget: under $500
